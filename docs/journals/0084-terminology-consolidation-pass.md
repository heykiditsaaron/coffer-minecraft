# 0084 Terminology Consolidation Pass

## Summary

Apply a minimal terminology-consolidation pass to reduce ambiguity around
contact, participation, omission, refusal, and boundary ownership.

This is a precision pass, not a rewrite and not a doctrine formalization.

## Clarifications Applied

- `contact` was made slightly more explicit as something that must be
  distinguished from mere interpretation.
- `participation` was tightened so it is not silently conflated with outcome.
- `non-success contact` was clarified as `non-success boundary contact` where
  that phrasing helped preserve the boundary distinction.
- The checklist now asks whether contact actually occurred or whether a note is
  only interpreting symptoms.
- The checklist now explicitly distinguishes what was reached from what was
  merely inferred.

## Why This Was Worth Doing

The recent geometry audit showed that the repository is already structurally
aligned, but a few terms still carry enough ambiguity to invite future
misreading.

The goal was to reduce that drift without collapsing the historical discovery
tone or over-normalizing the language.

## Left Untouched

- The SER/CER envelope wording.
- The current journal history and its exploratory tone.
- The distinction between Coffer-intrinsic law and container-boundary
  limitation.
- The mutation-seam deferral itself.
- Any attempt to rewrite Core Law or turn current interpretations into final
  doctrine.

## Rationale

The repository benefits from a little more terminology precision at the
high-value ambiguity points, but it does not benefit from a wholesale
terminology regime.

The current wording still needs to preserve reconstructability and the fact that
these are best-fit interpretations from an ongoing boundary audit.

## Scope

Included:

- a few targeted wording adjustments
- the ambiguity areas that motivated them
- the explicit decision to avoid broad rewriting

Excluded:

- code changes
- schema changes
- tests
- gameplay
- commands
- UX
- schema expansion
- final terminology authority claims

## Verification

No implementation changed for this pass.

## Uncertainties

Future boundary work may reveal more places where a similar small clarification
is justified, but that should be decided case by case rather than by a broad
rewrite sweep.
